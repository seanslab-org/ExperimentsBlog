# seanslab Blog Series: Invisible Infrastructure

## Published

1. **FFmpeg: The Code That Runs Every Video on Earth** — Bellard, Niedermayer, the fork war, the Hall of Shame, and billions of dollars built on unpaid code.
2. **WebRTC: The Protocol That Made the Browser a Phone** — Google's $68M GIPS acquisition, the codec war, SDP vs ORTC, and how it became pandemic infrastructure.

## Upcoming

3. **Audio Codecs: From Telephone to Opus** — G.711 (1972, PCM for PSTN) → G.728/G.729 (CELP, telecom compression) → MP3 (Brandenburg, Fraunhofer, psychoacoustics, Suzanne Vega's "Tom's Diner", Napster) → AAC (Dolby/Fraunhofer, iTunes) → Vorbis/FLAC (Xiph.org, open-source rebellion) → SILK (Skype) → Opus (SILK + CELT merger, the codec that ended the war). Patents, politics, and signal processing from 64 kbps toll-quality to transparent audio at 32 kbps.
4. **Video Codecs: Compressing Light** — BMP → JPEG (DCT, the transform that started it all) → MPEG-1/2 (motion compensation, I/P/B frames) → H.264 (the codec that won everything, MPEG-LA's patent empire) → VP8/VP9 (Google's open-source play) → H.265/HEVC (patent pool chaos, three competing licensors) → AV1 (Alliance for Open Media, royalty-free, the codec war's endgame). How we went from raw pixels to streaming 4K on a phone.
5. **Wireless: Taming the Electromagnetic Spectrum** — Shannon's theorem → Marconi → Bell Labs → AMPS/GSM → Qualcomm's CDMA bet (and winning) → Wi-Fi (CSIRO patent fight) → Bluetooth → LTE → 5G. Spread spectrum, OFDM, MIMO, beamforming, spectrum auctions, and the Huawei geopolitical war.
6. **ARM** — Sophie Wilson, Acorn Computers, the RISC bet nobody asked for, Apple nearly killing it then saving it, and how a chip designed for a BBC Micro successor ended up in every phone on Earth.
7. **CUDA: Jensen's Bet That GPUs Could Think** — 2006, NVIDIA lets programmers write general-purpose code on graphics cards. The industry laughs. Then machine learning happens. From GeForce to H100, $10B to $3T, and why one company owns the entire AI training stack.

## Concept Essays

8. **How Everything Talks: Communication Beyond Humans** — Mycorrhizal networks (the "wood wide web"), chemical signaling in flowers (volatile organic compounds to attract pollinators, warn neighbors), quorum sensing in bacteria, whale song across ocean basins, bee waggle dances, ant pheromone trails. Communication is older than language, older than brains, older than animals.
9. **Bandwidth: The Universal Bottleneck** — Shannon's channel capacity → PCIe lanes → human attention span → a highway at rush hour → how many words per minute you can read → synaptic throughput → the bandwidth of a fiber optic cable vs. a truck full of hard drives. One concept, a dozen fields, always the same constraint: how much can flow through a pipe per unit time.
10. **Resolution: How Fine Can You See?** — Pixel density → optical diffraction limits → the Planck length → geological time vs. human history (million-year bins vs. minute-by-minute Twitter) → ADC bit depth → medical imaging → the resolution of memory itself. Why every measurement is a tradeoff between detail and scope.
